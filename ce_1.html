Contrastive / Similar Examples

This prototype displays a to-be-assessed insurance claim alongside historical cases that are similar in profile. These historical cases are categorized into those previously deemed fraudulent and those not. This contrastive approach helps users assess the current case by comparison to known outcomes.


Evaluated Prototype – User Feedback

Users found the presentation titles too generic and the key numerical details hard to scan quickly. As a result, the important contrasts were harder to interpret at a glance.


Improved Version – Design Iteration

  • Font sizing and hierarchy were applied to better distinguish content types, allowing users to quickly orient themselves and compare examples.
  • Important numbers and claim details were emphasized to enhance visibility and ease of comparison.
  • Elements with the most relevance—like fraud status and key differences—were made more prominent to create clear visual anchors or “view hooks.”
